Reading Sharding

发表于2023-08-03

《The old man and the sea》

  A man can be destroyed but not defeated.

  Why did they make birds so delicate and fine as those sea swallows when the ocean can be so cruel?

  He took all his pain and what was left of his strength and his long gone pride and he put it against the fish’s agony and the fish came over onto his side and swam gently on his side, his bill almost touching the planking of the skiff and started to pass the boat, long, deep, wide, silver and barred with purple and interminable in the water.

《The little prince》

  ”The thing that is important is the thing that is not seen…”

  I ought to have guessed all the affection that lay behind her poor little stratagems.

  ”If some one loves a flower, of which just one single blossom grows in all the millions and millions of stars, it is enough to make him happy just to look at the stars. He can say to himself, ‘Somewhere, my flower is there…’ But if the sheep eats the flower, in one moment all his stars will be darkened… And you think that is not important!”

《Alice’s adventures in wonderland》

  ’I quite agree with you,’ said the Duchess; ‘and the moral of that is-“Be what you would seem to be”-or if you’d like it put more simply-“Never imagine yourself not to be otherwise than what it might appear to others that what you were or might have been was not otherwise than what you had been would have appeared to them to be otherwise.”‘

《The wonderful wizard of OZ》

  They had taken the sparkle from her eyes and left them a sober gray; they had taken the red from her cheeks and lips, and they were gray also.

  A little way off was a small brook, rushing and sparkling along between green banks, and murmuring in a voice very grateful to a little girl who had lived so long on the dry, gray prairies.

  ”That is because you have no brains” answered the girl.”No matter how dreary and gray our homes are, we people of flesh and blood would rather live there than in any other country, be it ever so beautiful.There is no place like home.”

《Little women》

  Very few letters were written in those hard times that were not touching, especially those which fathers sent home.

  It was like Marmee to get up a little treat for them, but anything so fine as this was unheard of since the departed days of plenty.

  ”If Marmee shook her fist instead of kissing her hand to us, it would serve us right, for more ungrateful wretches than we are were never seen,” cried Jo, taking a remorseful satisfaction in the snowy walk and bitter wind.
